What is IISER?

The Indian Institutes of Science Education and Research (IISERs) are a group of autonomous institutions that focus on imparting and promoting science and research education in India. These institutes were established by the Ministry of Education, Government of India.

What is the IISER Exam?

The IISER Aptitude Test (IAT) is a national-level entrance exam conducted for admission to 5-Year BS-MS Dual Degree, 4-Year BS (IISER Bhopal & IISER Tirupati), and B.Tech (IISER Bhopal) programmes.

Is IISER better than NISER?

The answer depends on a student’s educational aspirations and career goals. Those interested in pure or applied sciences and planning to build a career in research should consider IISER. On the other hand, students who wish to focus on atomic energy and related fields should apply to NISER through the NEST exam.

As per the latest research rankings, IISERs—particularly IISER Kolkata and IISER Pune—are considered better than NISER. However, if affordability is a major concern, NISER offers a more economical option.

Is IISER better than IITs?

The IITs primarily focus on developing engineering competencies and innovation skills. Therefore, students aiming to pursue careers in engineering, research, and development should choose IITs

In contrast, IISERs are ideal for students who wish to pursue PhD-level research and work at leading research institutes. When it comes to affordability, IISERs are generally more cost-effective than IITs.

IISER Eligibility Criteria 2026

Only candidates who meet the IISER Aptitude Test (IAT) 2026 eligibility criteria can apply for IAT 2026. The overview of the exam eligibility criteria has been provided as follows:

  • Candidates must have passed Class XII (or equivalent) in 2024 or 2025 or 2026 from an institute recognised by the Council of Boards of School Education (COBSE) in India.
  • Candidates must be from the science stream and have at least three subjects among Physics, Chemistry, Biology and Mathematics in Class XII.
  • Indian nationals, PIO/OCI holders, and foreign nationals are allowed to apply for IISER 2026.
  • Foreign applicants must upload an equivalence certificate issued by AIU unless their board is COBSE recognised.

IISER Application Process 2026

The application form for IISER 2026 will be released in March 2026 (tentative) on the official IISER website – iiseradmission.in. The application process will only be available in the online mode. Candidates can check the application steps mentioned as follows:

  1. Go to the official website – iiseradmission.in.
  2. Fill in the required details: email ID and phone number and generate a password to create a login account.
  3. Enter your educational details and personal details.
  4. Upload your passport-size photo and a scanned copy of your signature.
  5. Pay the application fee online and submit the form.

IISER Preparation Tips 2026

Candidates must have a preparation strategy to score well in IISER 2026. To achieve this objective, they can use the following preparation tips:

  1. Know the entire IISER Aptitude Test syllabus (Physics, Chemistry, Math, Biology).
  2. Learn NCERT books well for good fundamentals.
  3. Practice previous year papers and mock tests regularly.
  4. Conceptual clarity rather than learning by heart.
  5. Improve problem-solving and numerical abilities.
  6. Stick to a time schedule with good time management.
  7. Revising using short notes and key formulas.
  8. Recognise weak subjects/topics and work on those.
  9. Stay healthy, confident, and stress-free during study.

IISER Counselling 2026

The IISER 2026 counselling will commence after the announcement of IISER Aptitude Test (IAT) 2026 results. Shortlisted candidates who pass the exam will be called for counselling and seat allocation.

At the time of counselling, students must register online, enter their choice of IISERs and courses, submit documents, and pay the counselling fee. Seat allocation will be made on the basis of rank, choice, and vacant seats at IISERs for the candidate. The final admission will only be provided after proper document verification and payment of fees.
