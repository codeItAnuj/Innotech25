What is Volleyball?

Volleyball is a non-contact team sport played by two teams of six players on a court separated by a net. The objective is to score points by grounding a ball on the opposing team's side of the court. Teams are allowed a maximum of three touches to return the ball across the net. It is defined by its core actions: the bump (pass), set, and spike (attack), demanding precise coordination and explosive athleticism.

History and Origin

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan at a YMCA in Holyoke, Massachusetts, USA. Originally called "Mintonette," Morgan sought to create a less strenuous, less physically demanding sport than basketball, suitable for older members of the YMCA. He combined elements of tennis, baseball, handball, and basketball.

The name was quickly changed to "Volley Ball" after a spectator noted the volleying nature of the game. It spread rapidly through the YMCA network worldwide. The sport made its official debut as a medal event in the 1964 Tokyo Olympic Games, solidifying its global appeal. üéØ Basic Rules and Scoring

  • Scoring (Rally Point): A point is scored on every rally, regardless of which team served.
  • Match Structure: A match is typically the best of five sets. The first four sets are played to 25 points, requiring a two-point margin.
  • Deciding Set (Tie-Break): The final fifth set (if needed) is played to 15 points, also requiring a two-point margin. There is no point cap in volleyball.
  • Three Touches Rule: A team is allowed a maximum of **three consecutive contacts with the ball to return it over the net (the block does not count as a contact).
  • Rotations: In Indoor Volleyball, players must rotate one position clockwise after winning the right to serve following a point scored by the opposing team.
  • Net Faults: It is a fault if a player touches the net during the action of playing the ball, or if any part of the body crosses the centerline under the net, interfering with the opponent.
  • The Libero: A specialized defensive player who wears a contrasting uniform and is limited to playing in the back row. They cannot serve, block, or attack the ball from above the height of the net.
